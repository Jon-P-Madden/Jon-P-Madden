## Make Me! — AI-Assisted Career Tools Suite

I'm building **Make Me!** — a suite of AI-assisted career tools designed around the full job search lifecycle, not just a single step. Built on the Anthropic Claude API as a browser-first prototype suite.

Most career tools focus on activity tracking or resume formatting. **Make Me! focuses on interpretation and execution** — helping job seekers turn raw experience into clear positioning, sharper applications, stronger interviews, and more confident negotiations.

### The Six-Engine Roadmap

| Engine | Status | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| 1 — [Resume Positioning Engine](https://github.com/Jon-P-Madden/resume-positioning-engine) | ✅ Built | Turn raw career history into a strategically positioned resume |
| 2 — [Application Engine](https://github.com/Jon-P-Madden/application-engine) | ✅ Built | Analyze job postings, tailor application materials, generate cover letters, track applications |
| 3 — LinkedIn Optimization Engine | ✅ Built | Optimize LinkedIn profiles for recruiter searchability and credibility |
| 4 — Interview Prep Engine | 🔲 Planned | Generate likely questions, STAR stories, and role-specific prep |
| 5 — Salary Negotiation Engine | 🔲 Planned | Compensation strategy, counteroffers, and negotiation messaging |
| 6 — Recruiter Outreach Engine | 🔲 Planned | Recruiter, referral, and networking outreach |

> Position → Apply → Optimize → Prep → Negotiate → Outreach.
> A complete job search lifecycle, in one suite.

## Why This Exists

I built Make Me! to solve a real problem I lived through. Most job search tools assume you already know how to position yourself — they only help you format what you already have, or track what you've already done.

Make Me! starts earlier and goes further. It interprets your experience, surfaces what matters for a target role, helps you write the materials, prepares you for what comes next, and stays with you through outreach, interviews, and offers.

This isn't a portfolio project. It's a product thesis: that AI-assisted career tools should support the whole job search, not just one slice of it.
